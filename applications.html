Applications

Neurodegeneration is a natural first proving ground

Many neurodegenerative diseases are tightly linked to pathological protein misfolding and aggregation. That makes them a compelling first application lens for Emergent: the biology is structural, the failure modes are molecular, and the unmet need is enormous.

Misfolding focus

Alzheimer's, Parkinson's, and related disorders

These diseases do not reduce to one broken protein, but abnormal folding and aggregation are central enough to define the target selection logic.

Disease biology

Why misfolding matters here

Alzheimer's disease is strongly associated with beta-amyloid plaques between neurons and tau tangles inside them. Parkinson's disease is strongly associated with abnormal alpha-synuclein and Lewy bodies in affected brain cells. In both cases, pathological protein behavior is part of the disease signature rather than a side detail.

Alzheimer's

Two hallmark protein pathologies

Extracellular signal Beta-amyloid plaques
Intracellular signal Tau tangles
Why it matters Cell communication breaks down

The disease is strongly associated with protein aggregates both between neurons and inside them, making structural failure part of the core pathology.

Parkinson's

Aggregation around alpha-synuclein

Protein signal Misfolded alpha-synuclein
Cellular finding Lewy bodies
Why it matters Vulnerable neurons become dysfunctional

Parkinson's disease has become a model case for how abnormal protein behavior can be tracked, detected, and potentially targeted as part of disease biology.

Alzheimer's disease

The Alzheimer's Association describes plaques and tangles as prime suspects in damaging and killing nerve cells. Plaques are beta-amyloid deposits that build up between nerve cells, while tangles are twisted tau fibers that build up inside cells.

Parkinson's disease

NINDS notes that affected brain cells in Parkinson's disease contain Lewy bodies, which are deposits of alpha-synuclein. Misfolded alpha-synuclein is now important enough that seed amplification assays are being developed to detect it.

Why this fits Emergent

If disease depends in part on whether proteins fold, aggregate, or destabilize, then mutation-aware and structure-aware models become directly relevant to target selection and design strategy.

Program directions

How a protein design company could engage these diseases

Aggregation-aware mutation ranking

Prioritize variants and structural regions that appear most likely to destabilize a fold or increase pathological aggregation risk.

Stability-driven design exploration

Search for sequence changes that preserve desired function while reducing the structural fragility that allows misfolding to cascade.

Biotech and pharma partnership tools

Build model-assisted workflows that help disease teams interpret mutation data, triage candidates, and define better experimental follow-up.
