Science

Sequence alone is too thin a view of a protein

Emergent is built around a research thesis: to predict protein dysfunction and eventually design better proteins, models must reason across sequence, structure, and mutation effects together.

Scientific thesis

Sequence + structure + function

Biological meaning sits in the interaction between these layers, not in any one of them by itself.

Research principles

What the company believes technically

Proteins are relational objects

Residues matter because of their neighborhoods, contacts, and long-range effects on the fold, not just because of local sequence identity.

Mutation is an information-rich probe

Harmful substitutions expose which bonds, motifs, and spatial regions carry the most functional weight.

Embedding spaces should stay biochemical

A useful model organizes residues by physically meaningful behavior, not just by statistical similarity.

Prediction is only the midpoint

The destination is design: stabilized proteins, lower aggregation risk, and stronger therapeutic hypotheses.

Method arc

How the science progresses

01

Start with known proteins and measured mutations

Sequence, structural annotation, and mutation outcomes define a training problem that is grounded in real biological failure, not abstract language modeling alone.

02

Learn a structure-aware representation

Graph message passing lets the model absorb both peptide connectivity and residue proximity in three-dimensional space.

03

Use mutation damage as a guide to design

Once fragile regions are visible, the next question is how to search for variants that keep the same function with less structural risk.

Research questions

What the next experiments should answer

Can richer structural inputs improve mutation ranking again?

The early GNN result suggests yes, but new architectures should test how much more geometry and interaction detail can help.

Which embeddings transfer best into generation?

If the representation really captures stability and damage, it should become a useful prior for proposing new sequences.

How close can computation get to wet-lab decisions?

The practical test is whether the model can narrow the experimental search space in a way biologists trust.
